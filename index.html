NGO Heritage Case Study

Repositioning a UN Agency for a New Strategic Plan

Client Context

A UN development agency operating across 12 Nigerian states needed to align its visual identity with a new five-year strategic plan focused on youth employment and digital transformation.

The Challenge

The existing brand felt dated and donor-focused. The agency needed to signal relevance to young Nigerians while maintaining credibility with government partners and international funders.

The Approach

We conducted stakeholder interviews across three states, audited all existing brand touchpoints, and developed a dual-audience identity system — vibrant and forward-facing for youth communications; restrained and institutional for government and donor materials.

The Deliverables

Complete visual identity system, brand guidelines (68 pages), stationery suite, report templates, social media toolkit, event branding for national launch, training session for 24 communications staff.

The Outcome

Brand system adopted across all 12 state offices. Launch event materials praised by visiting UN Under-Secretary. 40% reduction in design turnaround time for state communications teams.
