Our Story

Colours and Codes was founded on a simple observation: Nigeria's most important institutions deserved brand communications that matched their significance. Not templated work. Not "good enough for Africa." Work that could stand beside the world's best — because it was built to the same standard.

We began by serving the organisations with the highest standards: United Nations agencies operating in Nigeria. The work demanded compliance with global brand guidelines, production at institutional scale, and stakeholder management across government, diplomatic, and community contexts. We delivered. Repeatedly.

Today, we bring that same discipline to Abuja's corporate sector — to law firms competing for federal contracts, to real estate developers launching landmark properties, to multinationals establishing their Nigerian presence, and to government agencies communicating with citizens.

We are based in Abuja because we believe Nigeria's capital deserves a creative partner that understands its unique institutional culture — the formality of the Three Arms Zone, the diplomacy of Maitama, the commerce of the Central Business District. We are Nigerians, designing for Nigeria, benchmarked against the world.
